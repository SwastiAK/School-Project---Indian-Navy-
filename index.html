The Indian Navy



The Indian Navy is the maritime branch of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Navy. The Chief of Naval Staff, a four-star admiral, commands the navy. As a blue-water navy, it operates significantly in the Persian Gulf Region, the Horn of Africa, the Strait of Malacca, and routinely conducts anti-piracy operations and partners with other navies in the region. It also conducts routine two to three month-long deployments in the South and East China seas as well as the western Mediterranean sea simultaneously.







 

Role Of Indian Navy

The full range of operations in which a nation's naval forces may be involved is vast, ranging from high intensity war fighting at one end to humanitarian assistance and disaster relief operations at the other. This broad continuum of operations can be broken down into distinct roles, each demanding a specific approach to the conduct of operations.

Indian Navy Achievements

Indian Navy is one of the three pillars of India’s military might. It has not only helped India win several wars but it has also been a part of major relief operations including– Operation Sukoon, in which the Navy evacuated Indian, Sri Lankan, Nepalese and Lebanese nationals during 2006 Lebanese war and Operation Raahat wherein the Navy safely evacuated over 5000 people from the war-torn Yemen. Every year, December 4 is celebrated as the Navy Day to honour the achievements and commemorate the countless sacrifices made by the Navy personnel on various occasions.
